Welcome to the 2025- Spring CSC + ENGR Co-op (W-1) Module X! 

Daphne Sidaway-Wolf – Sessional Instructor

 

This course will give engineering and computer science students an opportunity to learn more about two of Engineers Canada’s Graduate Attributes- Professionalism and the Impact of Engineering on Society and the Environment- as well as about Health and Safety, Risk Assessment, Sustainability, Environmental Management and more.

I hope you have some fun with this course and with the co-op work experience. It is a great time to find out what you like doing- and what you don’t. It is a gift to be able to do work terms- though there might be days when it might not seem quite that way.

A bit of background: I am a Licensed Professional Engineer, currently the Chair of Engineers and Geoscientists BC, Victoria Branch, and have served on the Executive for over 13 years. I am the immediate past Director, Food Safety in the Health Protection Branch (8 years) of the BC Ministry of Health. I have managed branches of up to 30 technical and policy people, and have worked in the provincial and federal departments of Agriculture (Research Branch in Ottawa to start), McGill University and in exploration bush camps in northern Canada. The commute from our fly-in tent camp to the ‘field’ was by helicopter.

I have hired many people including several UVic coops, and helped others find opportunities to grow in new jobs. I like to help people succeed.

My family moved overseas right after I finished high school and I know what it can be like fitting into a new country, language and educational system. To those of you new to Canada- kudos for being here and all the best!
